FEVER AND MALARIA — MOHFW STG
Temperature above 37.5°C is fever.
Treatment: Paracetamol 15mg/kg every 4-6 hours.
Malaria RDT positive for Plasmodium vivax: Treat with Chloroquine 25 mg/kg body weight divided over 3 days (10 mg/kg on day 1 and 2, 5 mg/kg on day 3) plus Primaquine 0.25 mg/kg daily for 14 days. Avoid Primaquine in pregnancy.
Danger signs requiring URGENT REFERRAL: severe malaria, high parasitaemia, unconsciousness, convulsions.

DIARRHOEA AND DEHYDRATION — MOHFW STG
How to prepare ORS: dissolve 1 sachet of Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) in 1 litre of clean drinking water.
Treatment for a child with diarrhoea: Give 200ml of prepared ORS after each loose stool.
Zinc supplementation: 20mg daily for 14 days for children above 6 months.
Continue breastfeeding. Continue feeding.
Danger signs requiring URGENT REFERRAL: sunken eyes, no urine for 6 hours, blood in stool, unable to drink, unconscious.

ACUTE RESPIRATORY INFECTION — MOHFW STG
Mild: home treatment, paracetamol, fluids, monitor.
Severe (chest indrawing, stridor, cyanosis): URGENT REFERRAL immediately.
Pneumonia: Amoxicillin 40mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses for 5 days.

OBSTETRIC EMERGENCIES AND ANTENATAL CARE — MOHFW MCH PROTOCOL
Danger signs in pregnancy: vaginal bleeding, severe headache, blurred vision, swelling of face and hands, BP > 140/90.
Severe pre-eclampsia/eclampsia (BP > 160/110 with headache): REFERRAL NEEDED immediately to CHC or District Hospital.

DIABETIC EMERGENCIES — MOHFW NCD PROTOCOL
Normal blood glucose is 70-140 mg/dL.
Hypoglycemia (Blood glucose < 70 mg/dL): If conscious, give oral glucose or sugar water.
If patient is unconscious and blood glucose is low (e.g., 38 mg/dL): URGENT REFERRAL needed immediately. Do not give oral fluids to unconscious patient.
